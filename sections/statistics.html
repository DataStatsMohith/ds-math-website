Concept 01
Probability Axioms
Probability formalises uncertainty. The three Kolmogorov axioms define the rules every probability model must follow.
$$P(A) \in [0,1], \quad P(\Omega) = 1, \quad P(A \cup B) = P(A)+P(B)$$

Concept 02
Normal Distribution
The bell curve appears everywhere in nature and statistics. Defined by mean μ and variance σ², it's central to the CLT.
$$f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}}$$

Bayes' Theorem
Updating beliefs with evidence. The foundation of Bayesian inference, spam filters, medical diagnosis, and much more.
$$P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A)\,P(A)}{P(B)}$$

Concept 04
Expectation & Variance
Expectation is the weighted average; variance measures spread. Together they fully characterise a distribution's shape.
$$E[X]=\sum x\,p(x), \quad \text{Var}(X)=E[(X-\mu)^2]$$

Step through the derivation of Bayes' Theorem
🧩 Deriving Bayes' Theorem
Step 1 / 5
  1. 1
    Joint Probability
    The probability of both A and B occurring is the joint probability P(A∩B).
    $$P(A \cap B) = P(A,B)$$
  2. 2
    Chain Rule (two ways)
    We can factor the joint probability using conditional probability in two different orderings.
    $$P(A,B) = P(A|B)\,P(B) = P(B|A)\,P(A)$$
  3. 3
    Set Equal
    Since both expressions equal P(A,B), we can set them equal to each other.
    $$P(A|B)\,P(B) = P(B|A)\,P(A)$$
  4. 4
    Solve for P(A|B)
    Divide both sides by P(B), assuming P(B) > 0, to isolate the posterior.
    $$P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A)\,P(A)}{P(B)}$$
  5. 5
    Named Components
    This is Bayes' Theorem. P(A|B) is the posterior, P(B|A) the likelihood, P(A) the prior, and P(B) the evidence.
    $$\underbrace{P(A|B)}_{\text{posterior}} = \frac{\overbrace{P(B|A)}^{\text{likelihood}}\;\overbrace{P(A)}^{\text{prior}}}{\underbrace{P(B)}_{\text{evidence}}}$$
